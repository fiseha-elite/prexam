Information System Hub

Bridging the gap between Business and Technology.

Information Systems (IS) is the study of complementary networks of hardware and software that people and organizations use to collect, filter, process, create, and distribute data.

  • 🏢 Strategic Alignment: Using IT to meet business goals.
  • 📈 Data-Driven Decisions: Turning raw data into actionable insights.
  • 🔄 Process Optimization: Improving organizational efficiency.
  • 👥 Socio-Technical Approach: Focusing on how people interact with tech.
  1. 📊 Systems Analysis and Design

    Learning how to analyze business requirements and design IT solutions that solve organizational problems.

  2. 🗄️ Database Management Systems

    Focusing on the administration, security, and architectural design of large-scale data storage.

  3. 💼 IT Project Management

    The methodologies (Agile, Scrum, Waterfall) used to lead technical teams and deliver software on time.

  4. 📉 Business Intelligence & Analytics

    Using tools like PowerBI, Tableau, or SQL to interpret complex data for stakeholders.

  5. 🛡️ Information Security & Governance

    Managing risks, compliance (GDPR/ISO), and the ethical use of corporate data.

  6. ☁️ Enterprise Architecture (ERP)

    Studying integrated systems like SAP or Oracle that manage all aspects of a business.

Unlike pure CS, IS focuses on the management of technology. Key theoretical concepts include:

  • 📑 Information Systems Strategy
  • 🔄 Change Management in Organizations
  • ⚖️ IT Ethics and Policy
  • 💰 Cost-Benefit Analysis of Systems

Practical sessions in Information Systems focus on implementation and modeling:

  • 🛠️ Database modeling and SQL querying
  • 🧩 Process mapping and Flowcharting (BPMN)
  • 📊 Data visualization workshops
  • 📱 Prototype designing (UI/UX for business apps)

An IS degree prepares you for a "Translator" role between nerds and suits:

  • 🗣️ Stakeholder communication
  • 🔍 Requirements gathering
  • 📉 Statistical data analysis
  • 🧩 Critical thinking and Business Logic
  • 🏗️ Enterprise resource planning
