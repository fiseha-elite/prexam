Active vs. Passive Voice

Elite writing is direct. Learn when to lead with the "doer" and when to focus on the "action."

The Passive Voice

The Receiver Leads

The subject is being acted upon. Often feels wordy or evasive.

[Object] + [Be-Verb] + [Past Participle]

"The exam was failed by the student."

The Active Voice

The Doer Leads

The subject performs the action. It is stronger, shorter, and clearer.

[Subject] + [Verb] + [Object]

"The student failed the exam."

Interactive Transformation

Flip the Sentence

"The groundbreaking research was conducted by the freshman team."

Current: Passive

💡 The Elite Exception

In Scientific writing, passive voice is often preferred to maintain objectivity (e.g., "The solution was heated to 100°C" instead of "I heated the solution").
